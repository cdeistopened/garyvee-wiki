# Platform Gap Analyzer

Identify which platforms you're missing and which you should prioritize based on your audience demographics and Gary's current platform attention valuations. Most creators are on 1-2 platforms. Gary says you should be on 7. This skill tells you which gaps matter most for YOUR audience.

## Context

Gary's personal brand in 2026 posts across 9 platforms with 57 handles, producing 400+ pieces of organic content per day. You're not Gary. But you ARE leaving money on the table if you're only on Instagram and TikTok while your audience is scrolling Facebook, LinkedIn, or Snapchat Spotlight with zero competition.

> "Almost everyone watching right now is so one or two-dimensional. Facebook, and I think there are people here that YouTube Shorts will be their breakthrough. I think LinkedIn is starting to get more and more obvious. I was wildly early on LinkedIn yelling and now that's getting more obvious." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`t6hw_llkIAc`)

> "Facebook proper, not Instagram, one could argue is the most important platform right this second because of how much attention is actually on it and how many people have forgotten about it because they've moved on to TikTok and Instagram. Facebook's sitting here with an ungodly amount of attention, especially if you're targeting 45 to 80 year olds." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`_TdzXnYJuSY`)

> "YouTube Shorts has never been more important to this room because the content you put out on YouTube Shorts is feeding the Gemini LLM, which is going to allow your product to show up when people type in [search query]. LinkedIn for B2B content. Snapchat Spotlight, if you're trying to reach 15 to 35, nobody's marketing in there." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Expo West 2026 (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

> "Nine platforms, 57 different handles, posting over 400 pieces of organic content a day." — Gary Vaynerchuk, describing his operation (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Map Current Presence

Ask:
1. **Which platforms are you currently active on?** (Active = posting at least 3x per week)
2. **How many posts per week on each?**
3. **What's your follower count on each?**
4. **What's your average engagement rate on each?**
5. **What content formats do you create?** (Video, written, image, carousel, audio, live)

### Step 2: Map Your Audience

Ask:
1. **What age range is your primary audience?** (e.g., 25-45)
2. **What's the gender split?** (If known)
3. **B2B or B2C?** (Or both?)
4. **What does your audience do for a living?** (Professionals, entrepreneurs, students, retirees)
5. **What are they searching for?** (Learning? Entertainment? Shopping? Connection?)

### Step 3: Cross-Reference Against Platform Demographics (2026)

| Platform | Primary Demo | Content Type | Discovery Model | Competition Level | Gary's 2026 Valuation |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Instagram** | 18-44, skews female | Reels, carousels, stories | Interest + follow-based | VERY HIGH | Fairly priced — everyone's here |
| **TikTok** | 16-35, broad | Short video, live, shop | Pure interest/algorithm | HIGH | Fairly priced for discovery, underpriced for live commerce |
| **YouTube Shorts** | 18-55, broad | Short video | Interest + search + AI feed | MEDIUM | **UNDERPRICED** — feeds Gemini/AEO, critical for search visibility |
| **YouTube Long-form** | 25-55, broad | Video 8-60 min | Search + subscribe + suggested | MEDIUM-HIGH | Fairly priced — high effort but high reward |
| **Facebook** | 35-80, broad | Video, images, groups, text | Interest + group-based | LOW (marketers left) | **MASSIVELY UNDERPRICED** — especially 45-80 demo |
| **LinkedIn** | 25-55, professionals | Text, carousels, video | Interest + network | MEDIUM (rising) | **UNDERPRICED** for B2B — getting more obvious |
| **Snapchat Spotlight** | 13-35, skews younger | Short video | Pure algorithm | VERY LOW | **UNDERPRICED** — almost zero brand competition |
| **X / Twitter** | 25-55, skews male, news-oriented | Text, threads, video | Interest + trending | MEDIUM | Fairly priced — depends on niche |
| **Substack / Beehiiv** | 25-55, knowledge-seekers | Long-form written | Email + recommendations | LOW | **UNDERPRICED** for thought leadership |
| **Pinterest** | 25-55, skews female, shopping intent | Images, pins, idea pins | Search + interest | LOW-MEDIUM | Underpriced for e-commerce and lifestyle |

### Step 4: Identify Gaps

For each platform the user is NOT on, score the opportunity:

| Factor | Weight | Scoring |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Audience match** | 40% | Is your target demo on this platform? (1-10) |
| **Competition gap** | 25% | How few competitors are posting here? (1-10, 10 = no competition) |
| **Format fit** | 20% | Can you easily adapt your existing content for this platform? (1-10) |
| **Gary's valuation** | 15% | How underpriced does Gary say this platform is right now? (1-10) |

**Weighted Opportunity Score = (Audience x 0.4) + (Competition x 0.25) + (Format x 0.2) + (Valuation x 0.15)**

### Step 5: Build the Entry Plan

For the top 2-3 gap platforms, provide:
1. **Why this platform for YOUR audience** — specific demographic overlap
2. **Content adaptation plan** — what existing content translates here
3. **First 5 posts** — specific post ideas native to this platform
4. **Posting cadence** — how many per week to start
5. **90-day milestone** — what success looks like on this platform in 3 months
