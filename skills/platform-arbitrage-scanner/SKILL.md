# Platform Arbitrage Scanner

Map your target audience to the platforms where attention is currently underpriced. Gary's career is built on spotting attention arbitrage: Google AdWords at $0.05/click in 2000, YouTube before anyone was watching in 2006, TikTok when it was Musical.ly, Facebook Blue when everyone moved to Instagram. This skill applies that lens to YOUR business right now.

## Context

Gary's entire philosophy is attention arbitrage. Find where the consumers are, where the marketers aren't, and go there before the price catches up.

> "Facebook proper, not Instagram, one could argue is the most important platform right this second because of how much attention is actually on it and how many people have forgotten about it because they've moved on to TikTok and Instagram. So, you have the best marketers putting all their energy on TikTok and Instagram. Meanwhile, Facebook's sitting here with an ungodly amount of attention, especially if you're targeting 45 to 80 year olds. Oh, by the way, they have plenty of money." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Day Trading Attention interview (`_TdzXnYJuSY`)

> "Snapchat Spotlight, if you're trying to reach 15 to 35, nobody's marketing in there because Snapchat's doing a terrible job promoting that they have a TikTok and Instagram. Even the most savvy in this room are not attacking there." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Expo West 2026 (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

> "In 1997, I had about 50,000 people on a newsletter and had 85% open rates. Google Adwords, the day it came out, I bought every wine term. They were five cents a click for the first six to 12 months." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Expo West 2026 (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

> "I was making videos on Musical.ly when it was me and a bunch of 14-year-old girls. It was creepy and I was scared I was going to get called out, but I knew TikTok was coming and I was prepared for the moment." — Gary Vaynerchuk, chiropractic keynote (`Sio8lkFXiMY`)

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Define Your Target

Ask:
1. **Who is your target customer?** (Age range, gender, interests, profession, geography)
2. **What platforms are you currently active on?** (And rough follower counts / engagement levels)
3. **What's your business model?** (B2B, B2C, e-commerce, local service, personal brand, creator)
4. **What's your content creation capacity?** (Can you do video? Written only? Have a team?)
5. **What's your paid media budget?** (If any — determines whether organic or paid arbitrage matters more)

### Step 3: Map Audience to Opportunity

Cross-reference the user's target demographic with the platform arbitrage map:

**Age-Based Routing:**
- **15-25:** Snapchat Spotlight (9/10) + TikTok (6/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10)
- **25-35:** TikTok (6/10) + Instagram (5/10) + Snapchat Spotlight (9/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10)
- **35-50:** Facebook Blue (10/10) + LinkedIn (8/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10) + Instagram (5/10)
- **50-80:** Facebook Blue (10/10) + YouTube (8/10) + Email/Substack (8/10)

**Business Model Routing:**
- **B2B:** LinkedIn (8/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10) + X/Twitter (6/10) + Substack (8/10)
- **B2C / E-commerce:** Facebook Blue (10/10) + TikTok Live (9/10) + Instagram (5/10) + Pinterest (7/10)
- **Local Service:** Facebook Blue (10/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10) + Google Business Profile
- **Personal Brand:** All platforms, weighted by content type preference
- **Creator:** TikTok (6/10) + YouTube Shorts (9/10) + Substack (8/10) + Instagram (5/10)

### Step 4: Build the Opportunity Map

For each recommended platform, assess:
- **Current presence:** Are they there? Active? Growing?
- **Competitive density:** How many competitors are actively posting there?
- **Content fit:** Can their existing content type work on this platform?
- **Effort to enter:** What would it take to start posting there?
- **Expected ROI timeline:** When would they see results? (Days, weeks, months)

Deliver a **Platform Arbitrage Report** in this format:

```markdown

**Business:** [name/type]
**Target audience:** [description]
**Current platforms:** [list with engagement levels]

## Opportunity Map

### 🔥 Highest Arbitrage (Start Here)
**[Platform]** — Arbitrage Score: [X]/10
- **Why now:** [specific reasoning from Gary's thesis]
- **Your audience there:** [assessment of whether target demo uses this platform]
- **Competition:** [how many competitors are active here]
- **Entry strategy:** [what to post first, how often, what format]
- **Expected timeline to results:** [X weeks/months]
- **Effort estimate:** [hours/week]

[Repeat for top 2-3 opportunities]

### ⚡ Moderate Arbitrage (Add When Ready)
**[Platform]** — Arbitrage Score: [X]/10
- [same structure, briefer]

### ⚠️ Overpriced (Reduce Investment or Skip)
**[Platform]** — Why overpriced: [reasoning]
- **Current ROI:** [their assessment]
- **Recommendation:** [maintain minimal presence / reduce posting / reallocate time]

## Action Plan
1. **This week:** Start posting on [platform] — [X] posts in [format]
2. **This month:** Establish cadence of [X] posts/day on [platform]
3. **Next quarter:** Add [platform] once [first platform] cadence is stable
4. **Ongoing:** Reassess platform arbitrage quarterly (attention shifts fast)

## The AEO/GEO Defensive Play
YouTube Shorts is non-negotiable regardless of your target demo. Every piece of content
you create is being indexed by Gemini to show up when people ask AI for recommendations.
This is your defense against disappearing in an AI-first search world.
```
