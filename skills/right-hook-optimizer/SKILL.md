# Right Hook Optimizer

When you throw a right hook — a sales post, a CTA, a promotion — make it land. This skill evaluates your planned or existing sales content for timing (have you earned the right to ask?), framing (does it serve the audience even while selling?), and CTA design (is the ask clear, direct, and easy to act on?).

## Context

Gary wrote Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook to stop people from selling in every post. But the book has a second lesson most people miss: when you DO throw the hook, throw it hard. A weak, apologetic right hook wastes the jab equity you built up. And never asking at all is just as broken as always asking.

> "Jab, jab, jab, right hook. Started writing for all of you in the world that were just selling in every post. And I was like, no, no, you have to give, give value. You know who ended up helping the most? The people that were scared to throw the right hook. The people that thought selling was yucky." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`rweBX3sO_8c`)

> "We all go through pendulums, right? I wrote a book called Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook. Get your ratio right." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

> "So put out more non-selling content. That's it. It really is. That's it." — Gary Vaynerchuk, diagnosing an affiliate marketer selling every day (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

The two failure modes:
1. **All hooks, no jabs:** You're the person Gary wrote the book for. Your audience feels used.
2. **All jabs, no hooks:** You're the person the book accidentally created. Your audience loves you but doesn't know what you sell.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Understand the Hook

Ask:
1. **What are you selling?** (Product, service, course, affiliate offer, event, etc.)
2. **Share the right hook post** — the sales content you want to optimize (or plan to create)
3. **What's your current jab/hook ratio?** (If they've run `content-ratio-auditor`, use that data. If not, ask for their last 5 posts before this hook.)
4. **What's your CTA?** (What exactly do you want the audience to do?)
5. **What platform is this for?** (Hook format varies dramatically by platform)

### Step 2: Evaluate Timing (Have You Earned This Hook?)

**The Jab Equity Check:**

Count the consecutive jabs BEFORE this right hook:

| Jab Count Before Hook | Timing Verdict |
|----------------------|----------------|
| **0 jabs** (hook after hook) | **NOT READY.** Post 3-4 jabs first. Your audience will ignore or resent this ask. |
| **1-2 jabs** | **Risky.** You might get away with it if the jabs were exceptional. Probably post 1-2 more. |
| **3-4 jabs** | **Good timing.** You've earned the ask. Throw the hook. |
| **5+ jabs** | **Overdue.** You've given enough value. The audience is warm. Ask now or you're leaving money on the table. |

**Context Timing Check:**
- Is there anything happening externally that makes this a good or bad time to sell? (Holiday, crisis, industry event)
- Is this a seasonal product? Timing the hook to seasonal demand multiplies impact.
- Did your last jab get high engagement? A hook right after a high-engagement jab benefits from audience attention.

### Step 3: Evaluate Framing (Does the Hook Serve Them?)

The best right hooks don't feel like selling because they still serve the audience. Rate the hook on:

| Framing Element | Score 1-5 | What 5 Looks Like |
|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Audience benefit is front and center** | | The first sentence is about THEIR problem, not your product |
| **Specific and concrete** | | "This course teaches you X, Y, Z" not "Transform your life" |
| **Social proof included** | | Real testimonial, specific result, or case study |
| **Urgency is real, not manufactured** | | Genuine deadline or limited quantity, not fake scarcity |
| **The ask is earned** | | Connection between the value you've given and what you're selling |

**Framing Score:**

| Score | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| **20-25** | Elite hook. Send it. |
| **15-19** | Good hook. Minor tweaks will help. |
| **10-14** | Mediocre hook. Rewrite with audience-first framing. |
| **5-9** | Bad hook. This is "BUY MY THING" energy. Complete rewrite needed. |

### Step 4: Evaluate CTA Design (Is the Ask Clear?)

The CTA must pass three tests:

1. **Single action:** One CTA per post. Not "follow, share, buy, and subscribe." ONE thing.
2. **Obvious next step:** "Click the link in bio" > "Check out my website" > "Learn more about what I do"
3. **Low friction:** Remove every possible barrier between interest and action.

**Platform-specific CTA best practices:**

| Platform | Best CTA Format | Worst CTA Format |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Instagram | "Link in bio" + story with swipe-up | "Go to my website dot com slash..." (no one types URLs) |
| TikTok | Comment a keyword for the link | Long verbal CTA at the end (they've already scrolled) |
| LinkedIn | Direct link in post (LinkedIn doesn't penalize links as much) | "DM me for details" (friction) |
| X/Twitter | Thread with link in last tweet | Link in first tweet (kills reach) |
| YouTube | Verbal CTA + card + description link | End-screen CTA only (most don't watch to the end) |
| Facebook | Direct link post or link in comments | "Link in bio" (Facebook doesn't have that) |

### Step 5: Rewrite the Hook

If the hook scores below 15 on framing, provide a complete rewrite that:
1. Leads with the audience's problem, not your solution
2. Includes one specific piece of social proof
3. Has a single, clear, low-friction CTA
4. Matches the platform's native format

## Output

Deliver a **Right Hook Assessment** in this format:

```markdown
# Right Hook Assessment
**Platform:** [platform]
**Product/Offer:** [what you're selling]
**Date:** [date]

## Timing Check
- **Consecutive jabs before this hook:** [X]
- **Timing verdict:** [Ready / Not ready / Overdue]
- **Recommendation:** [Post now / Post X more jabs first / Post immediately]

## Framing Score: [X]/25
| Element | Score | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Audience benefit first | [X]/5 | [note] |
| Specificity | [X]/5 | [note] |
| Social proof | [X]/5 | [note] |
| Real urgency | [X]/5 | [note] |
| Earned ask | [X]/5 | [note] |

## CTA Assessment
- **Single action:** [Yes/No] — [note]
- **Obvious next step:** [Yes/No] — [note]
- **Low friction:** [Yes/No] — [note]

## Current Hook
> [The hook as written]

## Optimized Hook
> [Rewritten version with improvements]

## What Changed and Why
1. [Change 1 — e.g., "Moved audience problem to first sentence"]
2. [Change 2 — e.g., "Added specific testimonial result"]
3. [Change 3 — e.g., "Simplified CTA to single action"]
```
