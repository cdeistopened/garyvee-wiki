# Content Ratio Auditor (Jab vs. Right Hook Classifier)

Take your last 15-20 posts and classify each as a jab (value, entertainment, education, inspiration) or a right hook (sale, CTA, link, promotion, brand deal). Calculate the ratio. Diagnose the imbalance. This is the quantitative foundation of Gary's most famous book concept.

## Context

Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook is Gary's 2013 book, and the concept remains the backbone of his content strategy advice a decade later. Jabs = content that serves the audience. Right hooks = content that asks the audience to do something for you (buy, click, sign up, share). The target ratio is roughly 3-4 jabs for every 1 right hook.

Most creators get the ratio wrong in one of two ways: they're all hooks (every post is selling), or they're all jabs (they never ask for anything).

> "I wrote a book called Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook. In 2013, the concept was give good content, give good content, give good content, and then occasionally ask for a sale in return. Get your ratio right." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

> "Jab, jab, jab, right hook. Started writing for all of you in the world that were just selling in every post. And I was like, no, no, you have to give, give value. You know who ended up helping the most? The people that were scared to throw the right hook. The people that thought selling was yucky." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`rweBX3sO_8c`)

> "A lot of affiliate marketing and I feel sometimes I'm selling and selling and selling every single day. So put out more non-selling content. That's it. It really is. That's it." — Gary Vaynerchuk to a creator (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Gather the Feed

Ask:
1. **Share your last 15-20 posts.** (Links, screenshots, or descriptions. 15 is the minimum for a meaningful audit — that's Gary's number.)
2. **Which platform?** (Classification rules differ slightly by platform)
3. **What are you selling?** (Product, service, course, brand deals, affiliate links — we need to know what a hook looks like for YOUR business)

### Step 2: Classify Each Post

For each post, assign one classification:

**JABS (serving the audience):**

| Jab Type | What It Looks Like | Example |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Educational** | Teaches something useful with no ask | "3 ways to improve your email subject lines" |
| **Entertaining** | Makes them laugh, feel, or think | A meme, a story, a hot take |
| **Inspirational** | Motivates without selling | Personal story of failure and recovery |
| **Community** | Engages them in conversation | "What's your biggest challenge this week?" |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Shows the human, not the product | Day in the life, office tour, team intro |

**RIGHT HOOKS (asking for something):**

| Hook Type | What It Looks Like | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Direct sale** | "Buy this" / "Link in bio" / product showcase | Product launch post with purchase CTA |
| **Lead capture** | "Sign up" / "Download" / "Join" | Free guide with email gate |
| **Brand deal** | Sponsored content, affiliate link | "#ad" or "#sponsored" post |
| **Self-promotion** | "Follow my other account" / "Listen to my podcast" | Cross-platform plug |
| **Engagement bait** | "Like if you agree" / "Tag a friend" | Designed to boost metrics, not serve audience |

**GRAY ZONE posts (classify case-by-case):**
- Testimonials: Jab if it teaches, hook if it's "look how great I am, buy from me"
- Case studies: Jab if it's genuinely educational, hook if it ends with a pitch
- "Value-first" sales posts: Hook. If the post ends with an ask, it's a hook regardless of how much value precedes it.

### Step 3: Calculate the Ratio

```
Jab Count: [X]
Hook Count: [X]
Ratio: [X]:1
Target: 3-4:1
```

**Diagnosis Table:**

| Ratio | Diagnosis | What's Happening |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **6:1 or higher** | **Hook-shy** — You're giving so much value you never ask. Your audience loves you but doesn't know what you sell. | You need MORE right hooks. Selling is not evil. You've earned the right to ask. |
| **4-6:1** | **Slightly generous** — Fine, but you could ask more often. | Consider adding one more hook per week. |
| **3-4:1** | **Target ratio** — You're in the sweet spot. | Maintain this. Focus on hook quality. |
| **2-3:1** | **Leaning salesy** — Your audience feels the pitch pressure. | Convert 2-3 hooks into jabs. Identify which hooks aren't earning. |
| **1-2:1** | **Overselling** — Every other post is an ask. Audience fatigue is real. | Major rebalance needed. Next 10 posts should be pure jabs. |
| **Below 1:1** | **All hooks** — You're treating your audience as a cash register. | Stop selling immediately. Rebuild trust with 15+ consecutive jabs. |

### Step 4: Post-by-Post Recommendations

For each hook that should have been a jab, provide a specific rewrite suggestion:
- What was the hook? What were you trying to get?
- How could you deliver the same VALUE without the ask?
- When SHOULD you throw that hook? (After how many jabs?)
