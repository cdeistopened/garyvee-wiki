# Selfless/Selfish Content Audit

Audit your last 15 posts through the selfless/selfish lens. This is sharper than the jab/hook distinction: a post can be a "jab" (no CTA) and still be selfish (exists to make YOU look good rather than serve THEM). Gary diagnoses creator plateaus in 5 seconds with this framework, and the answer is almost always the same: your content got selfish.

## Context

This is Gary's fastest diagnostic tool. He looks at someone's last 15 posts. If most of them are selfish — the creator wanted to look glamorous, wanted a brand deal to pay off, wanted the audience to buy something — he knows exactly why growth stalled.

> "The more you actually care about your content bringing value to your audience versus what it brings to you, the more you will grow. So many of you come to me on DM or in real life and are like, 'I've plateaued.' It takes me 5 seconds to look at your last 15 pieces of content and when it's 15 out of 15 that it was selfish for you — you wanted to look glamorous, you wanted them to buy something, you posted it because someone paid you — the fuck do you want from the audience, right?" — Gary Vaynerchuk (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

> "Almost everyone in this room plateaus when they're selfish. The selfish versus selfless framework gets out of whack. And to remind you all, you're all very fabulous, but there's other fabulous people. There's 8 billion people on Earth, and there's about to be a hundred billion fake people coming into the market every day. And if you're just trading on looking good, you're going to lose." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

> "When you are making decisions selfishly for your financial or insecurity or fame wanting, you will always lose. So the more you are selfless, the more you will win." — Gary Vaynerchuk (`yHjHVG6mBg0`)

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Gather 15 Posts

Ask:
1. **Share your last 15 posts.** (Links, screenshots, or descriptions. Exactly 15 — that's Gary's number.)
2. **For each post, tell me: why did you post it?** (The honest answer, not the polished one.)
3. **Are you currently in a growth plateau?** (Stagnant follower count, declining engagement, feeling stuck?)

### Step 2: Apply the Selfless/Selfish Lens

For each post, ask one question: **Who does this serve?**

**SELFLESS (serves the audience):**

| Selfless Signal | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| Teaches something the audience can USE today | "Here's the exact email template that got me a 40% reply rate" |
| Answers a question the audience actually asks | Responding to a common DM with a thorough answer |
| Entertains without requiring anything in return | A genuinely funny take on an industry problem |
| Shares a vulnerability that helps THEM, not just makes you relatable | "I failed at this and here's the specific lesson so you don't have to" |
| Gives away something that would normally cost money | A free template, checklist, framework |

**SELFISH (serves the creator):**

| Selfish Signal | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Exists to make you look successful | Flexing results without teaching how |
| Brand deal content that doesn't genuinely serve the audience | "#ad" for a product you'd never actually recommend |
| "Look at me" lifestyle content | Travel pics, fancy dinners, "grateful for this moment" without substance |
| Humble-bragging disguised as teaching | "When I hit $1M, I learned that..." (the point is the $1M, not the lesson) |
| Engagement bait with no value | "Like if you agree!" / "Tag someone who needs this!" |
| Content about YOUR feelings, YOUR journey, YOUR brand | "I'm so excited to announce..." (unless the announcement genuinely serves them) |

**THE GRAY ZONE — the hardest calls:**

| Post Type | Selfless Version | Selfish Version |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Personal story | Lesson-forward: the story serves the teaching | Story-forward: the teaching is an excuse to talk about yourself |
| Achievement post | "Here's exactly how I did it so you can too" | "Look what I did" with token advice at the end |
| Behind-the-scenes | Shows process so they can learn from it | Shows lifestyle so they can admire you |
| Testimonial | Teaches what you did that worked | Proves you're good so they'll buy |

### Step 3: Score and Diagnose

**The 15-Post Scorecard:**

| Post # | Selfless or Selfish | Why |
|--------|-------------------|-----|
| 1 | [S or s] | [1-sentence explanation] |
| 2 | [S or s] | ... |
| ... | ... | ... |
| 15 | [S or s] | ... |

**The Math:**

```
Selfless posts: [X] / 15
Selfish posts: [X] / 15
Ratio: [X]% selfless
```

**Diagnosis Table:**

| Selfless % | Diagnosis | Gary Would Say |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **80-100%** | **Genuinely generous** — Your audience feels served. Growth should follow. | "You're doing it right. Stay patient." |
| **60-80%** | **Mostly good** — A few selfish posts but the foundation is solid. | "Tighten up. Convert the selfish ones." |
| **40-60%** | **Drifting** — You're starting to post for yourself more than for them. | "You're losing them. Check yourself." |
| **20-40%** | **Selfish** — The plateau makes sense. Your audience feels used. | "This is why you're stuck. Flip it." |
| **0-20%** | **All about you** — Why would anyone follow? What value do they get? | "15 out of 15 selfish — the fuck do you want from the audience?" |

### Step 4: The Rewrite

For each selfish post, provide:
1. **What made it selfish:** The specific element that served you instead of them
2. **The selfless version:** How to make the same content serve the audience
3. **What to cut:** Sometimes the fix is deleting the self-serving element entirely
