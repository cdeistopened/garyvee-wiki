# Daily Content Calendar Builder

Build a realistic daily and weekly content posting cadence across Gary's 7 platforms, calibrated to team size, content creation capacity, and business goals.

## Context

Gary's thesis is not "post more." It is "post the right amount on the right platforms based on your resources, and never let time be an excuse."

> "Every Fortune 5000 company should have already posted three, six, 11 pictures or videos across the seven social networks and watched the organic AI-driven algorithms reach the audience based on the creative value." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Day Trading Attention interview (`_TdzXnYJuSY`)

> "How many pieces of content did you post in social yesterday? Six. Two. Ten. I posted 432." — Gary Vaynerchuk, chiropractic keynote (`Sio8lkFXiMY`)

> "If you have the audacity that you're trying to build something and you're going to tell me that you do not have time to create content to build brand and to grow your business, I don't even know what you're doing." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Expo West 2026 (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

The number is not 432. That is Gary with a team of 50+. The number is whatever is the maximum you can sustain at quality, across as many platforms as possible. The key word is sustain.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Assess Capacity

Ask:
1. **What's your team size for content?** (Solo / 1 person helping / small team of 2-5 / dedicated content team of 5+)
2. **How many hours per week can you spend on content creation?** (Be honest. Gary sleeps 7-9 hours. This is about efficiency, not burnout.)
3. **What content can you already produce?** (Can you do video? Write? Design graphics? Audio only?)
4. **What existing content do you have to repurpose?** (Pillar content, old videos, written material)
5. **What's your business type?** (B2B, B2C, personal brand, local business, e-commerce)
6. **What platforms are you currently on?** And which do you post to regularly?

### Step 2: Assign the Right Tier

| Tier | Team Size | Weekly Content Hours | Daily Post Target (all platforms combined) | Platforms |
|------|-----------|---------------------|------------------------------------------|-----------|
| **Starter** | Solo | 3-5 hrs/week | 3-5 posts/day | 3 platforms (TikTok + Instagram + 1 more) |
| **Builder** | 1-2 people | 8-15 hrs/week | 7-15 posts/day | 5 platforms |
| **Operator** | 3-5 people | 20-30 hrs/week | 15-30 posts/day | 7 platforms |
| **Machine** | 5+ dedicated | 40+ hrs/week | 30-100+ posts/day | 7-9 platforms, multiple handles |

**The non-negotiable minimum per Gary:** 3 pieces per day, across at least 3 platforms. If you're below that, you're not in the game.

### Step 3: Build the Calendar

**Platform Priority Order (2026):**

1. **YouTube Shorts** — Feeds Gemini AI. Long-term AEO/GEO defense. Non-negotiable.
2. **TikTok** — Largest discovery engine. Best organic reach for new audiences.
3. **Instagram** — Reels for reach, carousels for saves, stories for community.
4. **Facebook** — Massively underpriced for 35-80 demo. Most brands have abandoned it.
5. **LinkedIn** — B2B goldmine. Written posts and carousels dominate.
6. **X / Twitter** — Real-time conversation. Hot takes and threads.
7. **Snapchat Spotlight** — 15-35 demo, almost zero brand competition.

**Content Type Mix per Day:**

| Content Type | Purpose | Time to Create | Frequency |
|-------------|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Short video clip** (repurposed from pillar) | Reach, discovery | 15-30 min per clip | 1-5/day |
| **Quote graphic** | Visual, shareable | 5-10 min | 1-3/day |
| **Carousel / Slide deck** | Saves, education | 30-60 min | 1/day |
| **Text post** (LinkedIn, X, Facebook) | Thought leadership | 10-20 min | 1-3/day |
| **Story / ephemeral** | Community, behind-scenes | 5 min | 2-5/day |
| **Thread** (X, LinkedIn) | Deep tactical value | 20-40 min | 2-3/week |
| **Long-form written** (Substack, blog) | SEO, depth, email | 60-120 min | 1-2/week |
| **Live stream** | Community, discovery (TikTok Live) | 30-60 min | 1-3/week |

### Step 4: Build the Weekly Template

Provide a day-by-day template showing:
- Exact post count per platform
- Content type for each slot
- Estimated time to produce
- Which content is repurposed vs. original

Account for the efficiency insight Gary emphasizes:

> "So many people in this room could have so much more happen if they stopped making 15-minute meetings one hour." — Gary Vaynerchuk, Expo West 2026 (`VMumHwVEfFs`)

Content creation time is not the bottleneck. Decision-making time and perfectionism are. Batch production (shoot 5 videos in 1 hour) beats one-at-a-time creation.

```markdown
## Monday Schedule (Example Day)

| Time | Platform | Content Type | Source | Create Time |
|------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 8:00 AM | LinkedIn | Text post: [topic] | Original | 15 min |
| 8:30 AM | X | Thread: [topic] | Repurposed from [pillar] | 20 min |

## Batch Production Plan
- **Monday:** Shoot/record batch (3-5 videos in 1 hour)
- **Tuesday:** Edit + schedule the week's video content
- **Wednesday:** Write the week's text posts + captions
- **Thursday:** Create graphics + carousels
- **Friday:** Review performance, plan next week's pillar content

## Escalation Path
When ready to level up:
- Add [next platform] — estimated additional [X] hrs/week
- Add [content type] — estimated additional [X] hrs/week
- Hire first content person when hitting [X] hrs/week of content work
```
